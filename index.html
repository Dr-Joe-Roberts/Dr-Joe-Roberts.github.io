Dr Joe Roberts

I am a researcher and lecturer specializing in plant-insect interactions, sustainable pest management, and applied entomology at Harper Adams University. My work focuses on developing innovative solutions for agricultural pest management through the study of chemical ecology and insect behavior.

About Me

  • PhD in Invertebrate Chemical Ecology, with expertise in insect-plant interactions and behavioral analysis
  • Specialist in chemical ecology, insect behaviour, and the development of integrated pest management strategies
  • Lecturer at Harper Adams University, where I teach applied entomology and data science, bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical applications

Research Interests

Chemical Ecology

Investigating the chemical signals that mediate interactions between insects and their host plants.

Sustainable Pest Management

Developing environmentally friendly approaches to pest control in agricultural systems.

Data Science in Agriculture

Applying modern data analysis techniques to understand and predict pest population dynamics.

Teaching

At Harper Adams University, I lead courses in applied entomology and data science, focusing on:

  • Practical applications of entomological principles in agricultural settings
  • Modern data analysis techniques for agricultural research
  • Integration of sustainable pest management practices in commercial agriculture
